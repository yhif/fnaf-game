Series Overview

The Five Nights at Freddy's (FNAF) game series is a horror-themed franchise created by Scott Cawthon, first released on August 8, 2014. It has since become a global phenomenon with multiple sequels, novels, and merchandise. The series is set primarily in a fictional restaurant called Freddy Fazbear's Pizza, inspired by real-life family entertainment venues such as Chuck E. Cheese and ShowBiz Pizza Place.

"At Freddy Fazbear's Pizza, the animatronics are programmed to entertain during the day, but at night they roam freely. As a night security guard, your job is to survive five nights while monitoring these potentially dangerous animatronics."

Core Concept

  • Players usually assume the role of a night security guard at Freddy Fazbear's Pizza.
  • The gameplay involves monitoring security cameras, managing lights, doors, and ventilation to survive against hostile animatronic characters.
  • The story unfolds through voice recordings, mini-games, and in-game Easter eggs.

Timeline and Story Progression

The narrative timeline is complex and spans several decades, focusing on the dark history of Freddy Fazbear's Pizza and its animatronics. Key events include the founding of Freddy Fazbear Entertainment in the 1970s, multiple incidents of child murders, and hauntings by spirits possessing animatronics.

1970s and earlier

William Afton and Henry Emily develop entertainment animatronics; early conflicts begin.

1970s

Founding of Freddy Fazbear Entertainment and early restaurant openings; first child murder occurs.

1983

Brand consolidation under Freddy Fazbear and Friends; incidents like the "Bite of '83."

1987 and before

Multiple child deaths and disappearances; spirits possess animatronics; restaurant closures.

1993 and around

Restaurant reopens with old animatronics; William Afton killed by spring trap suit; spirits freed.

2023 and later

Freddy Fazbear's Pizza becomes a horror attraction; attempts to burn down the facility fail.

Expanded Universe and Spin-offs

The series includes spin-offs and collaborative games that explore alternate timelines and perspectives. These involve VR experiences, new antagonists, and expanded lore about the corporate and supernatural elements behind the restaurants.

Gameplay Elements

Survival Mechanics

Players must manage limited power resources while monitoring animatronic movements. Each night typically lasts from 12 AM to 6 AM, with increasing difficulty as the nights progress.

Animatronics

Each animatronic has unique behaviors and attack patterns. Some are more aggressive than others, and their movements can be tracked through security cameras.

Storytelling

Narrative is revealed indirectly through environmental clues, mini-games, and audio logs. The story is intentionally cryptic, encouraging fan theories and speculation.

Horror Atmosphere

Jump scares and suspenseful tension are central to the player experience. The games use sound design, limited visibility, and psychological horror to create a terrifying atmosphere.

Key Story Elements

The Afton Family

The Afton family, particularly William Afton (also known as the Purple Guy), is central to the series' lore. William is responsible for the murders of children at Freddy Fazbear's Pizza, which leads to the haunting of the animatronics.

The Missing Children Incident

Five children were lured to a back room by William Afton, who was wearing a Spring Bonnie suit. He murdered them and hid their bodies inside the animatronics, which later became possessed by their spirits.

The Bite of '83/'87

Two significant incidents in the series: the Bite of '83, where a child was bitten by Fredbear, and the Bite of '87, where an employee was bitten by an animatronic, leading to the removal of the animatronics' ability to walk during the day.

Circus Baby's Entertainment & Rental

A sister location to Freddy Fazbear's Pizza, featuring its own set of animatronics, including Circus Baby, who was designed to capture children.

Cultural Impact and Legacy

The FNAF series has inspired extensive fan theories, fan art, and fan-made games. It holds a Guinness World Record for the most sequels released in a year. The franchise has expanded into novels and merchandise, cementing its place in horror gaming culture.

Fan Community

The FNAF community is known for its dedication to solving the complex lore of the series. Theorists like MatPat from Game Theory have gained significant attention for their analysis of the games' story.

Expanded Media

Beyond the games, the FNAF universe has expanded into novels, graphic novels, and upcoming film adaptations, further enriching the franchise's lore and reach.
